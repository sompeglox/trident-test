What is a total solar eclipse?<\/strong><\/p>\n

A total solar eclipse occurs when the Moon passes directly between the Earth and the Sun, casting its shadow onto a narrow path on Earth\u2019s surface. In this region, known as the\u00a0path of totality<\/strong>, the Sun will be completely obscured for a short duration, ranging from a few seconds to several minutes.<\/p>\n

When and where to witness the eclipse?<\/strong><\/p>\n

The 2024 total solar eclipse will be visible across parts of\u00a0Mexico, the United States, and Canada<\/strong>. The path of totality will stretch from the Pacific coast of Mexico, travel through the central United States, and exit over the Atlantic coast of Canada.<\/p>\n
